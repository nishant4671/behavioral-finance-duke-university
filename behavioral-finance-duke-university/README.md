### 💡 Quick-Reference Core Concepts

1. **Rational Choice Model (Traditional Economics):** Assumes individuals are fully rational, have perfect information, process it without bias, and seek to maximize expected utility.
2. **Heuristics:** Fast, automatic mental shortcuts used to make quick decisions. Useful in daily life, but lead to systematic biases when applied to complex financial markets.
3. **Prospect Theory:** A behavioral model showing that people value gains and losses differently. We suffer more from a loss than we benefit from an equivalent gain (loss aversion), and we evaluate outcomes relative to a subjective reference point rather than absolute wealth.
4. **Nudges:** Positive reinforcement and indirect suggestions that can influence behavior and decision-making without restricting choices or changing economic incentives (e.g., auto-enrolling employees in retirement savings plans).
