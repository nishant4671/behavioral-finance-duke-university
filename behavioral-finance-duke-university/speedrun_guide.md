# Behavioral Finance (Duke University) Speed-Run Guide

If you want to complete this course as fast as possible (in 2 to 3 hours total), follow this exact playbook:

## ⚡ The 3-Step Speed-Run Playbook
1. **Test-First Strategy:** Try taking the quizzes first. If you pass, skip that section's lectures. If you miss a question, look up that exact term in the transcript.
2. **Read Transcripts, Don't Watch:** Reading text is 3x faster than watching video. Under each Coursera video, click the "Transcript" tab.
3. **Speed Playback:** If you do watch, set the playback speed to **1.5x or 2.0x**.

---

## 📖 Chapter, Video, and Quiz Breakdown

### 🔹 Module 1: Rational vs. Behavioral Economics (~40 mins)
* **Video Lectures:**
  * 📽️ *Welcome to Behavioral Finance* (Intro to instructors Emma Rasiel & John Forlines III).
  * 📽️ *The Classical Model & Rationality* (Core axioms: Completeness, Transitivity, Non-Satiation).
  * 📽️ *Utility of Money* (Decreasing marginal utility of wealth; risk aversion).
  * 📽️ *Expected Utility Theory* (How a rational agent calculates value: $\sum p_i \times u(x_i)$).
  * 📽️ *Real-World Deviations* (Introduction to Prospect Theory and Loss Aversion).
* **Exercises & Quizzes:**
  * 📝 **Week 1 Graded Quiz** (12 multiple-choice questions focusing on: rational utility axioms, risk aversion curves, and Expected Utility vs. Prospect Theory).

---

### 🔹 Module 2: Heuristics, Biases, and Probability (~50 mins)
* **Video Lectures:**
  * 📽️ *Probability Weighting* (How humans overweight small probabilities, e.g., lottery tickets, and underweight high probabilities).
  * 📽️ *Relative Probabilities* (Representativeness bias & base-rate fallacy).
  * 📽️ *Availability Heuristic* (Overestimating the likelihood of dramatic events like market crashes).
  * 📽️ *Anchoring & Adjustment* (Relying on arbitrary past numbers for valuation).
  * 📽️ *Mental Accounting* (Treating cash differently based on its origin, e.g., inheritance vs. salary).
  * 📽️ *Cognitive vs. Emotional Biases* (Loss aversion, confirmation bias, belief perseverance).
* **Exercises & Quizzes:**
  * 📝 **Week 2 Graded Quiz** (14 multiple-choice questions testing: representativeness, availability, anchoring, mental accounting, and probability weights).

---

### 🔹 Module 3: Financial Decision Making (~40 mins)
* **Video Lectures:**
  * 📽️ *Introduction to Financial Decisions* (Real-world consequences of biases).
  * 📽️ *The Disposition Effect* (Why we hold losers and sell winners: to avoid admitting we made a mistake).
  * 📽️ *Sunk Cost Fallacy & Overconfidence* (The impact on retirement plans and insurance choice).
  * 📽️ *Mitigation and Nudges* (Automated saving plans, behavioral rules of thumb, and default choices).
* **Exercises & Quizzes:**
  * 📝 **Week 3 Graded Quiz** (13 multiple-choice questions focusing on: the disposition effect, retirement auto-enrollment, and nudging methods).
