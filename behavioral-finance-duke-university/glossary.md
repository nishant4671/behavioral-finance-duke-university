# Plain-English Behavioral Finance Glossary
## glossary.md

*This database stores core psychological and behavioral economics terms mapped to PE/VC realities.*

---

| Term | Academic Definition | PE/VC Context & Actionable Mitigation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Prospect Theory** | A theory developed by Daniel Kahneman and Amos Tversky describing how individuals evaluate gains and losses relative to a reference point, showing that losses hurt roughly twice as much as gains please. | Founders will refuse a fair valuation down-round because it feels like a loss relative to their previous paper valuation (reference point). Mitigate by using structure (warrants, preferences) instead of changing the headline valuation. |
| **Anchoring** | A cognitive bias where an individual relies too heavily on an initial piece of information (the "anchor") when making decisions. | The investment bank’s teaser lists a target valuation of $150M. Your deal team builds an LBO model targeting $145M, even though standalone fundamental cash-flow analysis suggests the asset is worth $110M. Mitigate by building a "blind" valuation model before looking at the CIM. |
| **Herd Behavior (FOMO)** | The tendency of individuals to think, feel, or act in a way that mimics a larger group, rather than making decisions independently. | Bidding aggressively on a startup because "every Tier 1 fund is in the data room." Mitigate by enforcing a strict pre-mortem exercise: write the post-mortem of how the company failed BEFORE making the final investment. |
| **Winner's Curse** | A phenomenon in auction theory where the winning bidder tends to overpay for an asset because of incomplete information and competitive pressure. | Win a competitive bidding war for a mid-market manufacturing firm, only to find out you paid a 30% premium over fundamental value. Mitigate by setting a hard walk-away price at the Investment Committee meeting before the final bidding round. |
| **Narrative Fallacy** | Our limited ability to look at sequences of facts without weaving an explanation or story into them. | Backing a charismatic founder with an incredible storytelling style about "disrupting retail" while ignoring the fact that their customer acquisition cost (CAC) is higher than their lifetime value (LTV). Mitigate by separating pitch deck screening (story) from due diligence evaluation (pure data sheets). |
| **Escalation of Commitment (Sunk Cost Fallacy)** | A human behavior pattern in which an individual or group faces increasingly negative outcomes from a decision, yet continues the behavior instead of altering course. | Continuing to fund a failing Series A portfolio company because you've already invested $5M and want to "protect your investment," rather than redeploying capital to higher-performing assets. Mitigate by having a partner who is *not* on the deal board review the reinvestment decision. |
