## 📚 Module Breakdown

### 🔹 Module 1: Sourcing \& Screening (Utility, Sourcing Biases, \& FOMO)
* **Academic Core (Duke Syllabus):**
  * **Traditional Rationality:** The classical utility theory assumptions, rational axioms (Completeness, Transitivity, Non-Satiation, Continuity, Independence).
  * **Risk Attitudes:** Concave/convex utility curves, Expected Utility Theory ($E[U(X)] = \sum p_i U(x_i)$).
  * **Risk Aversion Coefficients:** Mathematical derivations of Arrow-Pratt Absolute Risk Aversion (ARA) and Relative Risk Aversion (RRA).
  * **The Behavioral Departure:** Introduction to Prospect Theory reference points and loss aversion.
* **Applied PE/VC Context:**
  * **Narrative Fallacy:** Sourcing deals based on compelling pitch deck storytelling rather than unit economics.
  * **Similarity Bias:** Homophilic network sourcing (funding founders with matching university/demographic backgrounds).
  * **Herd Behavior:** Sector FOMO (fear of missing out) driving herd-like capital allocation.
* **Milestone Project:**
  * **VC Deal-Screening Bias Audit Checklist:** A checklist designed to filter out narrative noise and benchmark valuation entry gates objectively.
* **Deliverable File:** `Module_1_Rational_vs_Behavioral_Economics/notes.md`

---

### 🔹 Module 2: Due Diligence (Heuristics, Overconfidence, \& Groupthink)
* **Academic Core (Duke Syllabus):**
  * **Cognitive Shortcuts:** The study of heuristics (Representativeness, Availability, Anchoring).
  * **Probability Misestimations:** Base-Rate Fallacy (Bayes' Theorem applications) and probability weighting.
  * **Overconfidence:** Calibration bias, optimistic forecasting, and the Planning Fallacy.
  * **Cognitive vs. Emotional Biases:** Information processing errors (correctable) vs. emotional feedback loops (accommodated).
  * **Belief Biases:** Confirmation Bias and Belief Perseverance.
* **Applied PE/VC Context:**
  * **LBO Model Optimism:** How the Planning Fallacy skews management LBO projections (underestimating synergy timelines, overestimating margins).
  * **Investment Committees:** Groupthink, dominant partner bias, and shared information bias in deal reviews.
  * **Diligence Blindsides:** Founder key-person risk and cognitive rigidity.
* **Milestone Project:**
  * **Founder \& Management Behavioral Risk Scorecard:** Qualitative screening matrix leveraging targeted interview questions to score management cognitive biases.
* **Deliverable File:** `Module_2_Heuristics_and_Biases/notes.md`

---

### 🔹 Module 3: Valuation \& Negotiation (Prospect Theory, Anchoring, \& Auction Dynamics)
* **Academic Core (Duke Syllabus):**
  * **Prospect Theory:** The S-shaped value function $v(x)$, diminishing sensitivity, and the loss aversion coefficient ($\lambda \approx 2.25$).
  * **Decision Weights:** The probability weighting function $\pi(p)$ (overweighting low probabilities, underweighting high probabilities).
  * **Risk Preference Quad:** The Fourfold Pattern of Risk Preferences.
  * **Cognitive Anchors:** Anchoring & Adjustment heuristic.
  * **Sunk Cost Fallacy:** Escalation of commitment under accumulated losses.
  * **Mental Accounting:** Segregation of capital accounts (fungibility violations).
  * **Myopic Loss Aversion:** Frequent performance evaluation leading to high risk premiums and low asset allocation.
* **Applied PE/VC Context:**
  * **M\&A Valuation Anchors:** How sell-side CIM targets skew bidding models.
  * **Confirmatory Diligence Sunk Costs:** Reluctance to walk away from deals after spending significant diligence capital.
  * **Liquidation Preferences:** Using structure (preferred returns, liquidation preferences) to bridge valuation expectation gaps.
  * **Auction Dynamics:** The Winner's Curse in highly competitive sponsor-to-sponsor buyout auctions.
* **Milestone Project:**
  * **PE Deal Bidding \& Negotiation Playbook:** Guidelines for deal teams during active negotiations, defining hard stop rules, walk-away parameters, and structural protection strategies.
* **Deliverable File:** `Module_3_Financial_Consequences/notes.md`

---

### 🔹 Module 4: Portfolio Operations (Choice Architecture, Nudges, \& Turnarounds)
* **Academic Core (Duke Syllabus):**
  * **Nudge Theory:** Principles of Choice Architecture (defaults, expecting error, structuring complex choices, incentives).
  * **Status Quo Bias:** Deep-seated resistance to change and structural re-organization.
  * **Loss Aversion in Organizations:** Framing losses vs. gains in change management.
* **Applied PE/VC Context:**
  * **Post-Close Value Creation:** Driving operational improvements (pricing model updates, ERP/CRM tool adoption) through choice architecture.
  * **Turnaround Management:** Structuring divestitures, plant closures, or executive pivots.
* **Milestone Project:**
  * **Portfolio Company Behavioral Intervention Blueprint:** An operational roadmap detailing Nudge-based incentive systems and choice architecture parameters.
* **Deliverable File:** `Module_4_Portfolio_Operations/notes.md`
